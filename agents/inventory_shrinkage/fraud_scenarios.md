\# Inventory Shrinkage Fraud Scenarios



\## Objective



This section illustrates realistic scenarios where inventory discrepancies may occur within grocery retail operations.



Inventory shrinkage can arise from operational errors, process weaknesses, or intentional misuse. While individual discrepancies may appear minor, recurring patterns can indicate deeper operational issues or potential fraud.



The following scenarios demonstrate how inventory shrinkage may emerge across retail operations.



---



\## Scenario 1: Delivery Quantity Discrepancies



Suppliers regularly deliver products to retail warehouses or stores. However, discrepancies may occur between the quantity delivered and the quantity recorded within inventory systems.



Example pattern:



\- Delivery records show lower quantities than expected.

\- Physical inventory counts consistently reveal missing units.

\- Certain suppliers or product categories exhibit repeated discrepancies.



Operational risk signal:



Repeated mismatches between delivery records and recorded inventory levels.



---



\## Scenario 2: Inventory Adjustment Manipulation



Inventory adjustments are often used to correct stock discrepancies during routine operations.



Example pattern:



\- A store records unusually frequent inventory adjustments.

\- Adjustments consistently reduce stock levels without clear operational explanation.

\- Adjustment activity is concentrated within certain product categories.



Operational risk signal:



Adjustment frequency significantly higher than expected operational benchmarks.



---



\## Scenario 3: Unusual Wastage Reporting



Retail stores frequently record wastage for perishable products that expire or become unsellable.



Example pattern:



\- Certain stores report unusually high wastage rates.

\- Wastage reporting spikes during specific shifts or time periods.

\- Wastage patterns differ significantly from comparable stores.



Operational risk signal:



Wastage reporting levels that deviate significantly from expected patterns.



---



\## Scenario 4: Inventory Discrepancies Between Sales and Stock Levels



Inventory systems track both product stock levels and recorded sales.



Example pattern:



\- Stock levels decline without corresponding sales activity.

\- Inventory counts reveal missing units despite normal sales records.

\- Discrepancies appear repeatedly for certain products.



Operational risk signal:



Inventory levels inconsistent with recorded transaction activity.



---



\## Scenario 5: Store-Level Shrinkage Patterns



Inventory shrinkage may be concentrated within specific stores or operational teams.



Example pattern:



\- Certain stores consistently report higher shrinkage rates than comparable locations.

\- Discrepancies occur across multiple product categories within the same store.

\- Inventory variances increase during specific operational periods.



Operational risk signal:



Shrinkage rates significantly higher than peer stores within the retail network.



---



\## Why Inventory Shrinkage Is Difficult to Detect



Shrinkage often becomes visible only during periodic stock counts or when discrepancies accumulate over time.



Because inventory moves through multiple operational processes — including receiving, shelving, sales, and wastage reporting — identifying the root cause of shrinkage can be difficult.



Continuous monitoring of inventory signals allows retailers to detect emerging discrepancies earlier.



---



\## Role of the Inventory Shrinkage Agent



The Inventory Shrinkage Agent continuously analyzes inventory movement signals across stores, suppliers, and product categories.



The system evaluates patterns such as:



\- delivery discrepancies

\- inventory adjustments

\- wastage reporting

\- stock levels relative to sales activity



By analyzing these signals together, the system can identify emerging shrinkage patterns and generate investigation insights before losses escalate.
