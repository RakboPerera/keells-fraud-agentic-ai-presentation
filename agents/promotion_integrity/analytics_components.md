\# Promotion Integrity Analytics Components



\## Analytical Objective



The Promotion Integrity Agent uses a combination of rule evaluation and behavioral analytics to identify unusual discount activity across retail transactions.



These analytical components allow the system to distinguish between legitimate promotional activity and patterns that may indicate misuse or operational manipulation.



Rather than relying solely on predefined rules, the platform evaluates behavioral signals across time, stores, and operational entities.



---



\## Promotion Rule Evaluation



Promotion campaigns are governed by defined eligibility rules.



These rules may include:



bundle purchase requirements  

minimum basket value conditions  

valid promotion periods  

eligible product categories  

customer or loyalty program eligibility  



The system continuously evaluates whether discounts applied during transactions align with these rules.



When promotions appear to be applied outside valid conditions, the system flags these events for further behavioral analysis.



---



\## Discount Pattern Analysis



Discount pattern analysis evaluates how promotions are used across transactions and entities.



The system analyzes patterns such as:



frequency of manual discounts  

distribution of discount values across items  

promotion usage across product categories  

discount activity across stores and shifts  



These patterns help establish a baseline of expected promotional behavior within the retail environment.



---



\## Peer Benchmarking



Peer benchmarking compares discount behavior across similar operational entities.



Examples include:



cashier-level discount activity compared with other cashiers in the same store  

store-level promotion activity compared with similar store formats  

shift-level promotion behavior compared with historical patterns  



This comparison helps highlight entities whose promotion activity deviates significantly from expected behavior.



---



\## Statistical Anomaly Detection



Statistical anomaly detection techniques identify unusual promotional activity within transaction data.



These methods may detect patterns such as:



unusually high discount frequency  

abnormally large discount values  

unexpected promotion usage patterns  

discount activity outside typical time windows  



These anomalies may signal potential misuse of promotion mechanisms.



---



\## Temporal Promotion Analysis



Temporal analysis evaluates how promotional activity evolves over time.



Examples include:



sudden increases in discount activity during certain shifts  

promotion usage spikes outside promotional campaign periods  

irregular timing of discount application across transactions  



Temporal signals help reveal coordinated or systematic promotion misuse.



---



\## Cross-Signal Correlation



Fraud patterns often emerge when multiple operational signals occur together.



The system therefore evaluates correlations between signals such as:



manual discount activity combined with override behavior  

promotion usage linked to specific cashiers or shifts  

discount activity associated with specific product categories  



Correlating these signals allows the platform to identify more complex promotion misuse patterns.



---



\## Investigation Intelligence Generation



When analytical components detect significant anomalies, the system generates structured investigation insights.



These insights summarize:



the promotional behavior detected  

the operational entities involved  

the supporting transaction signals  



Rather than presenting investigators with isolated alerts, the system provides contextual explanations that support effective investigation.



---



\## Analytical Outcome



By combining promotion rule evaluation, behavioral pattern analysis, peer benchmarking, and anomaly detection techniques, the Promotion Integrity Agent can identify promotion misuse patterns that would otherwise remain hidden within large volumes of transaction activity.



These analytical capabilities help retailers protect promotional investments while maintaining fair pricing practices.
