\# Supplier Risk Intelligence



\## Operational Context



Retailers rely on a network of suppliers to provide the products that stock store shelves and drive sales.



Suppliers deliver goods across multiple product categories and interact with retailers through procurement processes, pricing agreements, and delivery operations.



These interactions involve complex operational workflows including supplier onboarding, contract negotiation, pricing updates, and delivery verification.



Because supplier relationships span multiple operational processes, monitoring supplier activity for irregularities can be challenging.



Operational inconsistencies within procurement and supplier interactions may expose retailers to financial loss if not detected early.



---



\## The Operational Challenge



Supplier-related risks may emerge across multiple points within the procurement and supply chain process.



Examples include:



\- pricing discrepancies between agreed contracts and invoices  

\- unusual supplier pricing changes  

\- discrepancies between delivered and invoiced quantities  

\- irregular purchasing patterns across stores or categories  

\- concentration of purchases among specific suppliers  



These events may appear operationally valid when viewed individually.



However, when patterns are analyzed across time and across suppliers, unusual behaviors may become visible.



Because supplier activity spans multiple systems and processes, detecting these patterns manually can be difficult.



---



\## Signals Monitored



The Supplier Risk Agent continuously monitors operational signals related to procurement and supplier activity.



Key signals evaluated by the system include:



• supplier pricing patterns across product categories  

• purchase order quantities relative to historical purchasing behavior  

• invoice values compared with contract pricing  

• delivery quantities compared with purchase orders  

• supplier concentration within product categories  

• unusual purchasing patterns across stores or procurement teams  



By monitoring these signals across suppliers and procurement activity, the system can detect emerging supplier-related risks.



---



\## Supplier Risk Scenarios



Supplier-related risks may arise from a variety of operational situations.



Examples include:



Invoice values exceeding agreed contract pricing  

Delivery quantities inconsistent with purchase orders  

Unusual price increases for specific product categories  

Purchasing patterns concentrated around certain suppliers  

Repeated discrepancies between invoiced and received quantities  



While individual discrepancies may appear minor, repeated patterns may indicate operational weaknesses or potential procurement fraud.



---



\## The Role of the Supplier Risk Agent



The Supplier Risk Agent continuously analyzes procurement and supplier interaction data to identify patterns that deviate from expected behavior.



The agent evaluates signals across multiple operational systems including:



procurement systems  

supplier contract records  

purchase order systems  

delivery and receiving records  

inventory and product movement systems  



By analyzing these signals together, the system can identify irregular supplier activity that may require investigation.



---



\## Detection Objective



The objective of the Supplier Risk Agent is to detect unusual procurement patterns and supplier interactions that may expose the retailer to financial risk.



When anomalies are detected, the system generates investigation intelligence that highlights:



the supplier activity anomaly detected  

the suppliers, products, or procurement teams involved  

the operational signals associated with the activity  



These insights allow procurement and risk teams to investigate supplier-related discrepancies and strengthen procurement governance.



---



\## Role within the Fraud Intelligence Ecosystem



The Supplier Risk Agent operates alongside other analytical agents within the Fraud Intelligence Platform.



While this agent focuses on monitoring procurement and supplier interactions, other agents monitor complementary operational domains including transactions, promotions, inventory movement, and loyalty activity.



Together these agents provide a coordinated view of operational risk across the retail enterprise.
