\# Supplier Risk Agent Workflow



\## Workflow Objective



The Supplier Risk Agent analyzes procurement and supplier interaction data to identify irregular patterns that may expose the retailer to financial or operational risk.



Rather than evaluating supplier transactions in isolation, the system processes procurement data through a structured analytical workflow that evaluates pricing behavior, purchasing patterns, and supplier interactions across time.



This workflow transforms procurement activity data into actionable supplier risk intelligence.



---



\## Stage 1: Procurement and Supplier Data Ingestion



The workflow begins with the ingestion of procurement and supplier-related data from operational systems.



Relevant data elements may include:



supplier identifiers  

product identifiers  

purchase order records  

contract pricing agreements  

supplier invoices  

delivery and receiving records  

procurement approvals and modifications  

timestamps and operational metadata  



This data enables the system to observe how suppliers interact with the retailer across procurement processes.



---



\## Stage 2: Supplier Activity Signal Extraction



Once data is ingested, the agent extracts operational signals describing supplier interactions.



Examples of signals include:



supplier pricing patterns across product categories  

purchase order quantities across procurement teams  

invoice values compared with contract pricing  

delivery quantities relative to purchase orders  

supplier concentration within product categories  



These signals provide insight into how procurement activity is distributed across suppliers.



---



\## Stage 3: Procurement Pattern Modeling



The system analyzes procurement behavior across longer time horizons to establish baseline patterns.



Examples of baseline patterns include:



typical pricing levels across suppliers  

average purchase order quantities per product category  

normal supplier participation across procurement activity  

typical delivery quantities relative to orders  



These baselines represent expected procurement behavior within the retail supply chain.



---



\## Stage 4: Cross-System Reconciliation



The system compares signals across procurement systems to identify discrepancies.



Examples include:



invoice prices compared with contract pricing agreements  

delivery quantities compared with purchase order quantities  

procurement approvals compared with purchasing activity  



Cross-system reconciliation helps identify inconsistencies that may indicate supplier irregularities.



---



\## Stage 5: Supplier Benchmarking



To determine whether supplier behavior is unusual, the system compares suppliers against peers.



Examples include:



supplier pricing compared with similar suppliers in the same category  

delivery performance compared across suppliers  

supplier participation across procurement activity  



Benchmarking helps identify suppliers whose behavior deviates significantly from expected patterns.



---



\## Stage 6: Anomaly Detection



The system applies anomaly detection techniques to identify unusual procurement patterns.



Examples of anomalies include:



unusual supplier price increases  

repeated invoice discrepancies  

unexpected delivery shortfalls  

unusual supplier concentration within categories  



These deviations may indicate emerging supplier risks.



---



\## Stage 7: Investigation Intelligence Generation



When anomalies are detected, the system generates structured investigation insights.



These insights summarize:



the supplier activity anomaly detected  

the suppliers or products involved  

the operational signals associated with the anomaly  



These insights help procurement and risk teams investigate potential supplier irregularities.



---



\## Stage 8: Supplier Risk Monitoring Dashboard



The final stage surfaces investigation insights through supplier risk dashboards.



Procurement and risk teams can review:



suppliers exhibiting unusual pricing behavior  

products with repeated delivery discrepancies  

categories showing abnormal supplier concentration  

supporting procurement signals and patterns  



This enables teams to prioritize investigation and strengthen procurement governance.



---



\## Workflow Summary



The Supplier Risk Agent continuously analyzes procurement activity across suppliers and product categories.



By combining signal extraction, cross-system reconciliation, supplier benchmarking, and anomaly detection techniques, the system can identify supplier risks earlier and provide actionable intelligence for procurement oversight.
