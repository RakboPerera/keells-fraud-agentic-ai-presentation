\# Project Overview



\## Retail Operational Complexity



Modern grocery retail environments operate at an extraordinary level of operational complexity.  

Every day thousands of transactions occur across stores, inventory moves through supply chains, suppliers deliver goods, and promotional campaigns are executed across millions of customer baskets.



While these processes enable operational efficiency and customer convenience, they also introduce numerous points of vulnerability where fraud, misuse, and operational leakage may occur.



Across global retail markets, shrinkage and operational leakage typically range between \*\*1.5% and 2.5% of total revenue\*\*, representing one of the largest sources of preventable financial loss.



In grocery retail environments, these losses may originate from a combination of operational activities including:



• checkout manipulation and transaction irregularities  

• misuse of promotions and discounts  

• inventory discrepancies and shrinkage  

• supplier pricing or procurement irregularities  

• loyalty program misuse  



Because these risks occur across multiple operational systems, detecting them requires the ability to monitor patterns across large volumes of operational data.



---



\## Why Fraud Detection Is Difficult in Retail



Fraud detection in retail environments presents several structural challenges.



\### 1. Fraud rarely appears obvious in individual transactions



Most fraudulent activities appear legitimate when viewed in isolation.  

For example, a single price override or refund may be operationally justified. Fraud patterns typically emerge only when behaviour is analyzed across longer time horizons.



\### 2. Confirmed fraud labels are rare



Retail systems typically record operational events rather than confirmed fraud incidents. This limits the effectiveness of traditional supervised detection models.



\### 3. Fraud behaviours may span multiple systems



Operational signals may appear across multiple systems including POS transactions, inventory systems, procurement systems, and loyalty platforms.



Traditional rule-based monitoring systems struggle to identify patterns that span these domains.



---



\## The Opportunity for Agentic AI



Agentic AI introduces a new paradigm for fraud detection by deploying autonomous analytical agents that continuously monitor operational data streams.



Rather than relying solely on static rules or dashboards, these agents:



• observe operational events in real time  

• analyze behavioural patterns across large datasets  

• detect anomalies and deviations from expected behaviour  

• correlate signals across multiple operational systems  

• generate contextual investigation summaries  



Each agent is responsible for monitoring a specific operational domain within the retail value chain.



Together, these agents form a \*\*Fraud Risk Intelligence Ecosystem\*\* capable of identifying emerging operational risks and supporting investigation teams in prioritizing high-risk cases.

\## Demonstration Objective



This interactive presentation demonstrates how an \*\*Agentic AI Fraud Intelligence System\*\* could operate within a grocery retail environment such as Keells.



The demonstration focuses on five core fraud intelligence domains:



• Transaction Integrity Intelligence  

• Promotion and Discount Integrity Intelligence  

• Inventory and Shrinkage Intelligence  

• Procurement and Supplier Risk Intelligence  

• Nexus Loyalty Integrity Intelligence  



For each domain, the system illustrates:



• the operational problem  

• example fraud scenarios  

• the agentic AI workflow used to detect anomalies  

• the analytical components powering the detection system  



The goal is to show how Agentic AI can transform fraud monitoring from a reactive investigation process into a \*\*continuous intelligence capability embedded within retail operations\*\*.
